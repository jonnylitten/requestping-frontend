Frequently Asked Questions

Is anonymous FOIA filing legal?

Yes. Virginia FOIA law (Va. Code ¬ß 2.2-3700 et seq.) requires requests to be made by Virginia residents, but doesn't require the requester to be the end user. We're a Virginia resident filing on your behalf - completely legal and transparent. The agency knows it's RequestPing, but has no idea who you are.

How anonymous am I really?

Completely anonymous from everyone. Anyone looking at public FOIA records will only see "RequestPing, on behalf of a Virginia constituent" - no name, no email, no identifying information. Your identity is protected from agencies, the public, and anyone else who might search FOIA logs.

Why Virginia only?

Virginia FOIA law requires requests to be filed by Virginia residents. Since we're based in Virginia, we can legally file on your behalf. This residency requirement actually enhances privacy - we're not a national platform where patterns could be tracked.

Can you help with appeals?

We can provide general information about the appeals process, but custom appeal assistance may constitute legal advice. For complex appeals, we recommend consulting a FOIA attorney.
